Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā was developed by Sir Mason Durie in 1984. It's a metephor based on the four walls of a wharenui. Each wall represents a different dimension of health, we must look after each wall to ensure the structure stays strong. If one wall is damaged the whole structure is affected.

21 March 2025

The dimensions of Te Whare Tapa Whā

  • Taha Tinana (Physical Well-being)is all about caring for your physical body, whether that is with physical activity or eating foods the nourish your body.
  • Taha Whānau (Family & Social Well-being) is all about who helps you feel you belong and who you choose to share your life with - friends, family, work mates or your local community.
  • Taha Hinengaro (Mental & Emotional Well-being)is about how you think, feel and how you communicate. It's all about your mind and your heart.
  • Taha Wairua (Spiritual Well-being)explores the connection to your identity, spirituality, values and beliefs - these provide a sense of belonging. This can be viewed so differently by an individual, spirituality well-being can be having a belief in a higher power or it could be a connection to the universe, it's a truly personal element.
  • Whenua (Land, Environment, Connection to Place) is sometimes included to acknowledge the role of the natural world. It is all about your connection to the land. It includes plants, animals, bodies of water, and people. The whenua pillar can also be viewed as the place you feel most comfortable in - the place you belong. This is the foundation for the four walls - if you're missing a foundation, the walls have nothing to connect to!

Wellbeing plan

My well-being plan based off the four pillars off Te Whare Tapa Whā is detailed below.
Creating my wellbeing plan was straight forward for me because most of these activities are already things that are happening in my life. I think the hardest wall to strengthen, based on my activity choice, will be journalling each night and walking to the train station while conciously being in the moment.
Journalling at night is a good way to decompress about the day and it will allow my future self to look back on I've accomplished or to look at hasn't gone right so I can learn from it.
Walking to the train station while being consiously present might be a hard one to begin. It's muscle memory at this stage to chuck on my headphones, and play a podcast for my journey to work. My mornings are often filled with nervousness about the day ahead and it's easier to distract myself with a podcast than think about the day ahead. I plan to put my headphones on and not play anything until I get to the station - this way I can enjoy the sounds of the world around me, what a way to start the day!
