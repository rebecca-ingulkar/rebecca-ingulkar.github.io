Neuroplasticity & Growth Mindset

Learning at any age is totally possible, you just gotta put a little extra effort in.

21 March 2025

What are the key principles of Neuroplasticity?

  • Use it or lose it. The connection that your brain makes when you learn something new or practice a skill you already know strengthens the connection to that skill. If you stop practicing that skill, the connection will fade and practicing that skill at a overtime will become harder and you may feel like you need to start from the beginning again.
  • Repition and practice. Engaging in a practice of a skill repeatedly will strengthen the connection to that skill in your brain which in turn will make learning more effective.
  • Intensity and Challenge. While learning a new skill it is important to push yourself out of your comfort zome and engage with activities that require effort which will encourage stronger brain connections.
  • Novelty and complexity. Learning new information or completing new tasks stimulates our brain and develop plasticity that will develop our abiility to be adaptable.
  • Attention and focus. Ensuring we get ample amount of time of focused attention to learning, the deeper and more significant the changes will be to our brains function.

When you understand the principles of neuroplasticity it enhances your learning experience because it can boost your motivation to learn and you become aware of your own ability to learn. It also encuourages learning strategies such as spaced repition - this is when you study information over time to ensure the information is absorbed rather than forcing yourself to view alot of information in a small amount of time (which may have the opposite effect).
Additionally, having a grasp on the principles will show you that making mistakes is all part of the process, failing is a part of learning and making mistakes actually helps the brain grow.

TLDR; when you can understand how neuroplasticity works, you can stay motivated, use smarter science backed strategies for learning and take control of the outcome.

The way that I will use the princples of neuroplasticity for my benefit while studying with Dev Academy are as follows: I will use spaced repetition to reinforce the course work rather than cramming it in where possible. I'll remind myself that any effort to improve my skills leads to brain growth, embrace mistakes as they happen and learn from them - which will build resilence to future struggles. I will continue with my excercise habits as the principles of neuroplasticity suggest that regular physical activity helps with brain health. & I will continue with my meditation practice daily to assist with emotional regulation.

Ways to increase neuroplasticity:

  • Stimulate your brain by travelling/exploring a new country or suburb in your town, learning a new language or musical instrument or reading.
  • Exercise regularly. Regular physical exercise helps to prevent loss of connections in your brain which are linked to the part of your brain that stores your memory.
  • Practicing mindfulness can improve your neuroplasticity. By training your mind to focus on your body and allowing your mind to be free from thinking about the past or future, this helps to develop your emotional regulation, memory and .
  • Engaging in playing games, whether it's video or board games both can improve neuroplasticity. Games often builds on your problem solving skills, strengthening your prefrontal cortex whinh in turn improves your logical thinking. Gaming can strengthen the connections to the hippocampus to enhance your memory.

Growth Mindset

Growth mindset is the belief that your ability and intelligence can and will improve overtime with dedication, learning, effort. When you have a growth mindset you look at challenges as opportunties for growth and learning, you learn from your mistakes and you know that with effort you can improve. Growth mindset is relevant because it encourages lifelong learning, it increased motivation and resilience and it supports mental wellbeing.

The theory of growth mindset vs fixed mindset is something I have learnt before. But reasearching the topic for a decent period of time really helped me to understand myself a little bit more. I definitley grew up with a fixed mindset, I remember in highschool thinking that I wouldn't have much of an intellectual future because I didn't get good grades, and the thought "I'm either good at it or not" really determined my outlook of my future. Thankfully my mindset changed when I started to study as an adult and don't get me wrong, I'm still learning and trying to lean into the growth mindset side of things. I now know that learning doesn't stop once you finish high school, in fact your learning journey is only just beginning at that stage. My mindset has changed to "I can get better with practice"

To me the growth mindset is a mental game. It's all about self talk and how that affects me and my journey. I have written some of the words from growth mindset images on post-it notes and stuck them to the wall near my study space, so if the time comes that I need extra motivation to figure our how loops work, I can have a quick glance at the wall to shift my focus back to my learning goals and change my self-talk..

I recently read a book about neuroplasticity and the growth mindset written by David Eagleman, it's called "The Brain: The Story of you". I'm not a big reader, but this book is *chef's kiss*. Super fasinating and informative and backed up by science. If you can get a copy from your local library it's well worth the read.
